Audio Visualizations

As a kid, I loved listening to my music on Windows Media Player. Particularly, I enjoyed turning on the visualizer and watching the animations go by. Years later, I realized I don't see features like this on Spotify, so I went out to create my own solution.

I knew it was possible to retrieve audio spectrum data in Unity, but this requires that the music is loaded into your project. I wanted to visualize whatever is coming out of the sound card, so this was insufficient.

After some research, I found a great open-source audio library called CSCore which took care of most of my problems. I created a DLL of this library and adapted several of it's functions for use in Unity. Then, I created the graphical effects you see here. These are animated using data produced from a fast fourier transform of the audio coming out of your sound card.

Since this only depends on data from your sound card, it doesn't require songs to be loaded into Unity. You can simply stream music on Spotify with the visualizer open and watch what happens!
