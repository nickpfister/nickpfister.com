NOVASNAKE

TECH: Unity3D, ARCore, ARKit, PlayFab

NOVASNAKE remains one of my favorite achievements. It is a spin on the original snake/centipede games brought to life in 3D augmented reality. I worked on this alongside two talented interns to create a game that I, along with several others, just really wanted to play.

Though the concept of NOVASNAKE is relatively simple, there were several difficult problems we needed to solve in order to make a 3D AR snake game:

  • Filtering out noise/turbulence caused by player movement and imprecisions in AR tracking.
  • Easing players in to the unfamiliar feeling of collecting items in AR.
  • Collecting data on how players move in the real world while playing our game.
  • Writing cross platform AR code (this was before Unity's AR Foundations, so everything needed to be compatible with both ARKit and ARCore).

There were also a few non-AR related problems which NOVASNAKE tackled:

  • Creating online and offline modes, and merging data when players moved between them.
  • Creating an interface for developers to quickly add new abilities.
  • Adding rewarded advertisements which could be used to unlock new skins.
  • Using PlayFab to store player information and analytics.

NOVASNAKE was my first game to be released on both Android and iOS and make it beyond the testing phase. You can play it here:
