About me

You've found my website!

I've enjoyed creating things since I was a kid. My first step into development was creating mods for the original Halo: Combat Evolved back when I was 10 years old. In high school, I operated Minecraft servers full of custom mods which held up to 30 concurrent players.

I studied Astrophysics at UCLA for my undergrad. But while I was doing that, I found the itch to build software and games kept coming back. I often found myself creating game design documents and learning programming languages in my free time. And the linear algebra I learned in school ended up helping me the most in adding forces in Unity instead of writing academic papers.

Shortly after I graduated, I was accepted into the Startup UCLA Summer Accelerator with my company Planetary Gaming. This catapulted my career into a totally new territory where I found myself doing things I never could have imagined - from "speed dating" with local investors to giving presentations in front of 400 people to having a one-on-one meeting with Riot co-founder Marc Merrill.

Though my first company was not a commercial success, it helped solidify my career change away from academia and into the world of software. It also taught me far more than just good software architecture and design practices.

Right now, I'm looking for roles in software and/or games industries. My key interests include (but are not limited to):

  • AR/VR development (both practical and game-related)
  • Multiplayer networking
  • Graphics programming
  • Casual/mobile gaming
  • Data visualization
  • Education

If you're looking for a developer with my skill sets, I'd love to chat! Feel free to grab my resume and contact me using the links at the top of the site.
